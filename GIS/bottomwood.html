Potential Sites for Bottomland Hardwood, Schenck Forest

The Carl Alwin Schenck Memorial Forest is a reasearch forest for the Department of Forestry and Environmental Resources at NCSU, the area had many stands of hardwoods (deciduous trees) along stream corridors, these stands were lost to agricultural activties in the 1900's. The areas located in the map are sites of potential re-establisment with the following criteria:
1. Must be within 100 feet of streams
2. Fall within areas that are currently pine stands
3. Be on soil types suitable for Bottomland Hardwood growth
